About

Hi! I am Ashish Menon, a passionate researcher specializing in deep learning, computer vision, and machine learning. My work spans medical imaging to computational photography on edge devices, focusing on efficient AI solutions for real-world applications.

I completed my MS by Research at at CVIT, IIIT Hyderabad, under the guidance of Prof. C.V. Jawahar and Dr. Vinod P.K. , where I tackled critical challenges in the medical domain, such as data scarcity, lack of annotated data for cancer diagnosis, and interpreting cancer manifestations across multiple organs from a deep learning perspective. My research work resulted in two publications, a conference paper in interactive deep learning models to assist histopathology Whole slide image annotation and a journal on pan-cancer feature interpretation.

Currently at Qualcomm India R&D, I work under the guidance of Dr. Pawan Baheti , Dr. Hau Hwang towards deep learning-based image processing solutions for edge devices, optimizing models under power, area, and timing constraints. I work on ML R&D problems targetting perceptual human vision tasks in computational photography optimized for mobile camera systems (ISP). I design models to work on real time Image signal processor pipelines owning the end to end model design, quantization and Image Quality assesment. I closely collaborate with multiple teams to design optimized solutions and was also a part of the team that proposed first of its kind specialized hardware blocks to enable deep learning-based features on device. With Qualcomm I have two of works accepted in the internal flagship conferences, and a work considered for a US patent filing.

Research Aspirations

One of the primary directions I aspire to pursue is the development of data efficient models and their energy-efficient deployment, without compromising on explainability and interpretability. I believe this need is becoming increasingly relevant with the rapid rise of large-scale models in the fields of LLMs, VLMs, and generative learning. In the near future carbon footprint considerations could be envisioned as a key differentiator to selecting models. When combined with transparent model behavior and interpretability, these aspects can significantly contribute to community level trust and acceptance especially in high-impact domains such as medical AI, perceptual reasoning, and autonomous systems.

Interactive Learning for Assisting Whole Slide Image Annotations
Ashish Menon*, Piyush Singh, P.K Vinod, C.V. Jawahar
Asian Conference on Pattern Recognition
Springer / Paper / Slides /

This work demonstrates a novel semi supervised and active learning techniques using a retrieval based system to assist Annotations of gigapixel sized tissue slide images.

Exploring Histological Similarities across Organs from a deep learning perspective
Ashish Menon*, Piyush Singh*, P.K Vinod, C.V. Jawahar, Paper / Journal

This work brings to light similarities seen in the cancer manifestiations across organs from a deep learning perspective backed by solid proofs from GradCAM based interpretability and t-test based statistical studies.
